### 1. Linear Prediction Family (LPC, Reflection/PARCOR Coefficients, Residual, Formants, LPCC)
**Proposed note title:** `features/linear-predictive-coding-lpc-reflection-coefficients-formants-and-lpcc.md`

**Why high value:** MFCC note and pitch note explicitly compare against / reference LPC cepstra, LPC spectrum, reflection coefficients, and SIFT (LPC-inverse + ACF). Formant tracking is a recurring use case (vocal-tract resonances, secondary periodicity errors in pitch, subband energy for viz or control, whitening for robust pitch/flux). Lattice realization is *the* fixed-point-friendly, stable structure (|k_i| < 1 guarantees no limit-cycle growth); direct cross-ref to the lattice filters scaffold (reflection coeffs *are* the lattice k parameters). Levinson-Durbin is O(p²) per frame (p=10–16 for 8–16 kHz speech) with tiny working set (Toeplitz R + a vector); lattice recursion is O(p) and can be fused with pre-emphasis or whitening filter. Residual energy / prediction gain is an excellent "voicedness" or "predictability" scalar feature (cheap after LPC). LPCC (LPC → cepstra via recursion) is a direct historical competitor to MFCC; still used in some codecs and low-resource ASR. Elegant: PARCOR (partial correlation) coefficients are bounded [-1,1], quantize well (Q15 natural), map to lattice filter for synthesis or adaptive whitening with minimal state. Embedded relevance: speech codecs (historical LPC-10, modern hybrids), formant-based effects or visualizers, noise-robust front-ends (whitening before mel or pitch), vocal-tract parameter control for viz/synthesis.

**Must cover (per guidelines):** Mathematical definition (AR model, normal equations, Levinson-Durbin recursion, lattice step-up/step-down / Schur / reflection coeff extraction). Derivation of reflection coeffs from predictor or vice-versa; stability (|k|<1 ⇒ minimum-phase, bounded). First-principles traffic: O(p²) loads/stores for Levinson vs. O(p) for lattice update + filter; state = p coeffs + p filter delays (or shared with synthesis / whitening). Pseudocode for both Levinson and lattice analysis; fixed-point Q formats and conditioning (Durbin recursion sensitive to ill-conditioning; use double guard or block-floating; convergent rounding). Formant extraction: root-finding (lightweight for small p: Bairstow or Newton on polynomial) or peak-picking on LPC spectrum (evaluated via lattice or direct polynomial); sub-bin refinement via parabolic interp. LPCC recursion formula. Comparison table vs. MFCC (arithmetic intensity, state, noise robustness, formant fidelity, coding bit-rate); when LPC wins (formant tracking, low-bitrate coding, lattice impl simplicity, whitening). Hardware: CMSIS-DSP LPC? (limited or absent); NEON for dot products / Levinson accum; lattice as cascade of 1-multiplier sections (great for Helium/RVV vector of sections or scalar M4). Budgets: p=12 at 16 kHz / 10 ms frames; full voice front-end (pre-emph + LPC + residual energy + 4 tracked formants + pitch) state/traffic vs. MFCC path; interaction with lattice filters note. Cross-refs (bidirectional): MFCC (comparison + historical result), pitch (SIFT, formant errors in ACF, formant for voicing), filters lattice (exact k usage, limit-cycle suppression), numerical (quantization sensitivity, limit cycles in lattice synthesis), DWT (subband LPC for transients or coding), sliding-DFT (LPC on sparse spectrum for dominant resonances?), dominant-band (formant as "dominant resonance" tracker), perceptual-sparse (residual energy as predictability cue), dynamics (formant preservation in effects). 8–15+ verified primaries (e.g. Rabiner/Schafer "Digital Processing of Speech Signals", Makhoul, Atal/Hanauer, Markel/Gray lattice papers, Vaidyanathan filter banks).

**Elegant win:** A 12th-order lattice LPC analyzer + residual energy feature uses ~12–24 words state + one pass over a short autocorrelation buffer (or running ACF); the same k vector can drive a lattice synthesis filter or a whitening pre-filter for downstream pitch/flux/contrast with almost no extra traffic. One structure serves analysis, synthesis, and robustification.

### 2. Gammatone / ERB Auditory Filterbanks and GFCC / Auditory Cepstral Features
**Proposed note title:** `features/gammatone-erb-filterbanks-gfcc-and-auditory-cepstral-features.md`

**Why high value:** MFCC note repeatedly contrasts "triangular FFT-domain bank (cheap, one FFT + sparse dot-products)" vs. "gammatone or other IIR auditory filters (more faithful to critical-band integration, asymmetry, level dependence; M parallel filters, per-band state)". A dedicated note completes the comparison with actual numbers (traffic, state, fidelity, noise robustness). Canonical efficient digital realization is Slaney (Apple TR #35, 1993) — Patterson-Holdsworth auditory filter bank; typically 4th-order gammatone approximated by cascaded 2nd-order sections or direct recursive form with pre-computed poles/zeros per ERB center freq. GFCC (gammatone freq cepstral coeffs) and variants appear in modern comparative studies (often competitive or superior to MFCC on noise-robust or speaker tasks). CQCC (constant-Q cepstral) is the CQT analogue; natural companion to the CQT/NSGT note. Philosophy fit: direct time-domain filtering path (no FFT) can be lower latency, multiplier-light in fixed-point (or lattice/WDF), and maps to subband processing (cross DWT, wavelets for transients). Tradeoff is exactly the memory-traffic question: M independent IIR states (M×4–8 words) + M reads/writes per sample vs. one FFT working set + one sparse bank pass. Embedded: used in cochlear-implant models, low-power always-on front-ends; Slaney design is "efficient" by intent (fits the corpus title perfectly).

**Must cover:** Gammatone impulse response definition; ERB scale (Glasberg/Moore); design equations for center freqs, bandwidths, pole placement. Slaney efficient impl details (pole placement, cascaded biquads or 1-pole + correction term, coefficient tables or on-the-fly computation from ERB formulas; stability margins). GFCC pipeline: gammatone bank → envelopes (Hilbert or rectifier+LPF, or TEO approx for ultra-low) → power-law or log → DCT (or DCT on decimated subband powers). Traffic/state tables: direct IIR bank (per-sample: M×(loads of state + coeffs) + stores; working set M states + coeffs) vs. FFT+mel (N log N butterflies + sparse M). Working set for M=32–64 bands; opportunities for SoA layout + SIMD across bands. Fixed-point: coefficient sensitivity (gammatone poles near unit circle at low freq — use double-coeff or lattice/WDF forms for lower sensitivity?); dynamic range per band (headroom); decimation after filtering (polyphase or CIC cross-ref to resampling). Comparison: fidelity (critical-band integration, asymmetry, level dependence, masking) vs. compute/traffic; when GFCC wins (certain noise, music timbre discrimination, cochlear-like apps). Hardware: CMSIS or vendor auditory filterbanks (rare); NEON across independent biquads (SoA layout for vector load of states); Cortex-M4 scalar vs. Helium vector of sections or RVV. Budget example: 32-band GFCC front-end @16 kHz vs. 40-mel MFCC; total SRAM for coeffs (ROM) + state (RAM); fusion with ballistic envelopes / dominant / flux. Cross-refs: MFCC (the comparison table expanded + "when to choose direct IIR vs. FFT"), CQT (CQCC), DWT (subband energies vs. gammatone for transient vs. steady), filters (biquad/lattice/WDF realizations of the sections; minimal-state IIR), perceptual-sparse (envelopes + TEO on subbands; multi-band TEO), sliding-DFT (sparse auditory approximation?), dynamics (envelopes from subbands feed ballistics directly), resampling (decimation post-filter). Verified: Slaney 1993 (direct retrieval + PDF), Glasberg/Moore ERB papers, GFCC papers (e.g. Shao, Kim/Stern auditory-inspired), auditory model reviews.

**Elegant win:** Once poles are fixed, each gammatone section is a tiny autonomous IIR; with careful SoA layout the working set for 40 bands can stay in L1 or even registers for a block. Envelopes (or TEO) can feed directly into the same ballistic / dominant / flux / modulation machinery with zero spectrogram materialization and natural subband fusion.

### 3. Spectral Contrast (Octave-Based / Subband Peak-Valley) Features
**Proposed note title:** `features/spectral-contrast-octave-based-and-timbre-shape-features.md`

**Why high value:** Ubiquitous companion to MFCC + chroma in modern MIR, audio classification, and music-type papers (Jiang et al. 2002 "Music type classification by spectral contrast feature", IEEE ICME; cited 600+; implemented in librosa as `spectral_contrast`, Essentia, etc.). Algorithm is beautifully on-the-fly: partition current power spectrum into 6–8 (octave or log-spaced) sub-bands; per band compute peak (mean of top quantile, e.g. top 2–5% energy or simple max/mean of upper half) and valley (bottom quantile or min/mean lower); contrast = peak − valley (linear or dB). Optional valleys vector or DCT on contrasts for compact timbre descriptor. Discriminative power: high contrast → tonal/harmonic content (clear peaks); low contrast → noise-like or broadband. Excellent for music vs. speech, instrument family, genre, "brightness texture", "sharpness". Fits philosophy perfectly: same single pass over bins already loaded for flux, centroid, mel, dominant, HPS; state = small (6–8 contrast values + prior frame for delta-contrast if wanted); output is compact vector. Complements existing: centroid = energy-weighted mean (global "center of mass"); contrast = local peak/valley structure per critical band (local "tonality shape"). Low compute: quantile can be approximated by max + a few order stats or histogram binning (CLZ-friendly in fixed-point); no full sort needed for small bands (6–8). 

**Must cover:** Jiang 2002 definition + modern quantile variant (librosa default n_bands=6–7, quantile=0.02). Derivation: why peaks = harmonic/tonal, valleys = residual/noise; relation to spectral flatness (global) and harmonicity (pitch note). Pseudocode: bin grouping (octave boundaries or mel/CQT groups), per-group reduction (max + mean or partial sum for quantiles), contrast calc + optional post-processing. Traffic: **zero incremental** when fused with any other spectral reduction pass (mel, flux, centroid, dominant, chroma, HPS); only extra stores for the 6–8 scalars (+ optional deltas). Fixed-point: power → dB via table or approx; saturating accum; integer quantile via counts or selection network. Decision: contrast vector (6–8) + its mean/std or DCT coeffs as timbre descriptor; vs. full 13 MFCC for tinyML; when contrast adds unique value (music genre, tonal gating, particle "edge density" in viz). Applications: music classification, key-invariant timbre, gating "tonal" processing (e.g. cleaner pitch on high-contrast), richer viz mappings (contrast → sharpness, particle density). Cross-refs: MFCC (companion in feature vectors; expand comparison table), perceptual-sparse (flux/flatness/entropy family; **expand that scaffold with this** as a core member), CQT (natural log-freq subbands for octave contrast), dominant (peak picking related; subband dominant), pitch (harmonicity), chroma (both harmonic folding), DWT (contrast on detail vs. approx coeffs), loudness (contrast vs. overall level for "punch"). Verified primary: Jiang et al. 2002 (DOI 10.1109/ICME.2002.1035731; PDF retrieval + claim confirmation), subsequent MIR surveys (Tzanetakis, Peeters), librosa/Essentia impl notes (exact quantile defs).

**Elegant win:** "While the magnitude bins are still hot from the STFT or SDFT or CQT, a 20-line fused loop produces 13 mel + 12 chroma + 1 flux + 1 centroid + 6 contrasts + 1 dominant band index + HFC + entropy with only the compulsory loads of the bins themselves. The marginal cost of contrast is a few dozen extra adds/comparisons and 6–8 stores."

### 4. Perceptual & Integrated Loudness (ITU-R BS.1770 / EBU R128 Family)
**Proposed note title:** `features/perceptual-loudness-itu-bs1770-ebu-r128-streaming-measurement.md`

**Why high value:** Not the same as RMS or energy; industry-standard for broadcast, streaming, "loudness normalization" (Spotify, Apple, YouTube targets — e.g. -14 LUFS), production metering, and as a *reference level* for AGC/compressors (prevents pumping across genres). Exact signal chain: K-weighting pre-filter (high-pass + two shelving filters with published coeffs), mean-square integration over 400 ms blocks, gating (absolute threshold −70 LUFS, relative −10 dB below the loudest 10% of blocks), short-term / momentary / integrated / loudness range (LRA). Streaming / embedded version is non-trivial but low-state: IIR filter states (3–4 biquads), running sum-of-squares + count, circular or leaky block buffer for gating logic, FSM for "gated blocks" and integrated update. Perfect cross to dynamics note (the ballistic/AGC can target LUFS instead of raw RMS for perceptually stable [0,1] and cross-genre consistent viz/effects). Traffic: filters are per-sample (small working set, can fuse with other pre-emphasis); integration at block or control rate; no spectrogram. Verifiable primaries: ITU-R BS.1770-4 (or latest), EBU Tech 3341/3342/3343 docs (free PDFs, exact filter coeffs, gating pseudocode, tolerances).

**Must cover (highlights):** K-weighting derivation and exact biquad coeffs (fs-dependent or resampled from 48 kHz reference; cross filters note). Gated mean-square math; "loudness" in LUFS (log mapping + offset); short-term (3 s) vs. integrated. Streaming approximations (IIR power, overlapping blocks, histogram of block powers for relative gate or leaky max for "loudest 10%"). State machine for gating + integrated loudness update + LRA. Fixed-point: square → 64-bit accum (or block float), dB tables or log2 approx, careful with very low levels (dither/offset, denormals); headroom for K-filter overshoot. Traffic/state: 3–4 biquad states + ~8–16 words for power history/gating at 48 kHz; budgets for "always-on loudness meter + AGC" on Cortex-M (fits in DTCM with other O(1) state). Comparison: LUFS vs. A-weighted or simple RMS for music vs. speech; headroom implications (true-peak awareness); relation to crest. Cross-refs: dynamics (ballistic inputs can be LUFS-gated, AGC reference level, [0,1] scaling from loudness instead of raw), filters (the K biquads are textbook minimal-state IIR; exact coeffs), numerical (dB, dynamic range, log approx), MFCC (pre-emphasis similar but different weighting), perceptual-sparse (energy/crest companions; loudness as perceptual energy), dominant (loudness-weighted dominant?), modulation (loudness modulation depth). 8+ verified refs (ITU-R BS.1770 series, EBU Tech 334x, AES papers on real-time loudness, streaming adaptations).

**Elegant win:** One small filter cascade + gated accumulator turns raw samples (or pre-filtered power) into a perceptually meaningful control value that downstream AGC, viz, or effects can trust across genres and levels — without genre-specific tuning. Replaces ad-hoc RMS with a standard that "just works" for broadcast-grade consistency on embedded devices.

### 5. Modulation Spectrum, Subband Envelopes, and Rhythmic/Temporal Texture Features
**Proposed note title:** `features/modulation-spectrum-subband-envelopes-and-rhythmic-texture-features.md`

**Why high value:** Captures "texture" and rhythm (4–20 Hz modulation rates for fluctuation strength, roughness, beat-related groove) that frame-wise spectral stats (MFCC, contrast, centroid) miss. Typical pipeline: coarse subband decomposition (mel/octave/gammatone 4–12 bands), per-band envelope extraction (rectifier + ballistic LPF or Hilbert approx or TEO), then low-rate analysis of the envelope signal: FFT (tempogram/fluctuation pattern), ACF (periodicity), centroid of modulation spectrum, crest of envelope, zero-cross of differentiated envelope, etc. Rate: envelopes at 50–200 Hz (decimated), modulation analysis at 10–50 Hz → tiny state (small rings or recursive stats per band; e.g. 32–128 samples history at 20 Hz = <1 KiB total even for 8 bands). Fits "control-rate processors" (cross the dynamics note perfectly; envelopes already being computed); can be fused with existing envelope followers / ballistics. Applications: music genre/texture classification, voice quality (shimmer-like, roughness), automatic music/speech discrimination, richer reactive viz (modulation depth → particle "jitter" or "pulse" or multi-oscillator animation drivers), drum vs. tonal gating. Elegant low-compute variants: use the *same* ballistic filters already in the dynamics note; simple 4-band (bass/mid-high1/high2) for ultra-embedded; periodicity from zero-cross of differentiated envelope or TEO on envelope (no FFT). RASTA (RelAtive SpecTrAl) filtering is a classic linear modulation filter on log-mel or cepstra for channel-noise robustness; direct tie-in.

**Must cover:** Psychoacoustic motivation (Zwicker fluctuation/roughness, modulation filterbanks in auditory models). Envelope extraction options (cost vs. fidelity: rectifier+LPF cheapest, matches ballistic exactly; Hilbert more accurate but heavier; TEO approx). Modulation analysis: low-rate DFT on envelope ring (or Goertzel on candidate rhythm freqs), or simple stats (envelope crest, modulation centroid, energy in 4–8 Hz "beat" band via 2 Goertzels, ACF peak). State/traffic: per-band 1–2 words (envelope) + small modulation buffer (bytes at low rate); analysis cost only at control rate (60 Hz or slower); decimation considerations (anti-alias). Fixed-point: envelope scaling, decimation without aliasing, log for modulation spectrum. Decision framework: when full modulation FFT is worth it (genre/texture) vs. 2–3 simple stats (envelope crest, beat-band Goertzel, roughness proxy) for ultra-low. Cross-refs: dynamics (envelopes/ballistics are the front half; share state and time-constants), perceptual-sparse (TEO on subbands, flux as broadband modulation proxy, HFC), MFCC (RASTA is a form of modulation filtering on cepstra/log-mel; deltas are crude first-difference modulation), CQT (log-freq bands natural for rhythm), DWT (detail coeffs have high time-res for modulation), pitch (rhythm from inter-onset; groove affects pitch stability), dominant (subband dominant modulation), loudness (loudness modulation as "punch/groove" descriptor), sliding-DFT (low-rate modulation on envelopes via SDFT). Refs: Zwicker "Psychoacoustics", modulation spectrum papers (e.g. Sukittanon/Atlas, Schuller), rhythm feature surveys in ISMIR, RASTA papers (Hermansky).

**Elegant win:** The envelope states are *already being computed* for AGC or crest or loudness; adding 2–4 Goertzel or a tiny ACF on the downsampled envelopes costs O(1) extra per control frame and reveals "groove", "pulsiness", or "roughness" that pure spectral shape misses. One set of ballistic filters serves dynamics, loudness, modulation, and VAD.

### 6. Power-Normalized Cepstral Coefficients (PNCC) and Robust Auditory-Inspired Variants
**Proposed note title:** `features/power-normalized-cepstral-coefficients-pncc-and-robust-front-ends.md`

**Why high value:** PNCC (Kim & Stern, ICA 2012 / TASLP 2016) is a direct, well-studied robust competitor to MFCC that addresses power bias, noise, and channel effects via power-law nonlinearity (instead of log), medium-time power normalization (longer-term context), asymmetric noise suppression, and temporal masking. Appears in comparative studies as superior in real-world noisy conditions (exactly the deployment scenario for always-on embedded KWS / voice UI). Pipeline has more stages than classic MFCC → interesting traffic/state (extra running averages / IIRs for medium-time power, per-band noise floor trackers, masking state) but still fully on-the-fly after the core filterbank (gammatone or mel). Perfect complement to MFCC note: "when the extra state/traffic buys robustness". Ties directly to proposed GFCC (auditory filterbank) and modulation (medium-time is a form of modulation filtering). Embedded: fixed-point challenges in power norm, masking thresholds, and log/power-law approx are high-value to derive.

**Must cover:** Core PNCC stages (power-law, medium-time power, asymmetric suppression, temporal masking, DCT). Comparison traffic/state vs. MFCC (extra O(M) state for medium-time buffers or leaky trackers; extra passes or fused accum). Fixed-point recipes for power-law (table or approx), normalization without division bias, masking. When PNCC wins (noisy real-world vs. clean studio; resource-constrained devices where robustness > last 1–2% accuracy). Cross-refs: MFCC (direct competitor + expanded comparison table including PNCC), GFCC (PNCC often uses gammatone), modulation (medium-time / RASTA-like), perceptual-sparse (noise floor / gating synergies), dynamics (VAD freeze for normalization), numerical (bias, dynamic range in power domain), VAD (explicit integration). Verified: Kim & Stern papers (DOI 10.1109/TASLP.2016.2545928 and ICA12), follow-on robustness studies.

**Elegant win:** Medium-time normalization and asymmetric suppression can be implemented with small per-band IIRs or ring stats that live alongside the filterbank state; when the spectrum is already hot, the marginal cost is modest while delivering measurably better performance in the exact conditions (background noise, varying levels) that kill simple MFCC on embedded devices.

### 7–11. Additional High-Value Feature Proposals (Condensed)
- **RASTA and explicit modulation filtering on cepstra / log-mel** (expand or companion to modulation note): linear filters (typically IIR or FIR on modulation axis) to suppress convolutional/channel noise. Tiny state, huge robustness win in some domains. Cross MFCC, modulation, GFCC.
- **MPEG-7 Low-Level Audio Descriptors formalization** (AudioSpectrumCentroid/Spread/Flatness/Envelope, AudioHarmonicity, AudioPower, AudioFundamentalFrequency, etc.): many map 1:1 to existing (centroid, flatness, power, F0/harmonicity from pitch), but normative extraction (log-freq spectrum, specific smoothing) + traffic + fixed-point + "standard compliance on MCU" value. ISO/IEC 15938-4. Good for interoperability notes.
- **Phase / instantaneous-frequency / group-delay derived features & partial tracking** (group delay function, phase deviation/consistency for onsets/quality, inst-freq variance/histogram/vibrato, reassignment indicators or lite ridge extraction). Some coverage in STFT + perceptual + sliding-DFT + pitch (IF from phase diff); dedicated note for depth, traffic of extra phase passes or derivative computations, when worth it vs. plain mag features. Ties to advanced TF reassignment below.
- **Formant tracking with temporal continuity** (state machine / Kalman-lite on LPC roots or spectral peaks; 3–5 formant tracks with birth/death, continuity constraints). Per-frame LPC gives candidates; tracking turns them into stable control signals for viz/effects/synthesis. State ~ tens of words; huge for "vocal character" in reactive systems. Cross LPC, pitch (formant errors), dominant, dynamics (formant preservation).
- **Online / adaptive feature normalization and robust scaling** (running mean/variance per cepstral coeff or scalar feature, with VAD/energy gate to avoid silence bias; min/max trackers; per-band or global). Often the missing piece between raw features and tiny classifier or stable [0,1] control. Cross dynamics (freeze logic), MFCC/perceptual, VAD, numerical.

## High-Value Missing / Under-Covered Algorithms (Processing, Compositions, Detection, Effects)

These go beyond pure "extract scalar/vector while bins hot" into stateful streaming processors or source-separation / modification / effects that still must obey hard real-time, no-heap, bounded traffic, and explicit memory accounting. They are the "compositions" that every serious embedded audio engineer eventually ships.

### 1–6. Existing Proposals (Retained & Reinforced)
See current gaps for full detail (onset-beat-transient + VAD; HPSS streaming median strip or causal approx; partitioned NLMS/FDAF AEC with DTD reuse; Schroeder/FDN reverb with explicit delay-line read/write + matrix costs + modulation; PSOLA/WSOLA light with variable-hop rings + fractional interp; spectral subtraction / noise suppression + gating). These remain high-priority because their dominant costs are precisely the bytes moved (delay lines, strip buffers, long filter partitions, search windows) — perfect vehicles for the philosophy.

### 7. Feedback / Howl Suppression via Persistent Peak Tracking + Adaptive Notches
**Proposed:** `algorithms/feedback-and-howl-suppression-adaptive-notching.md`

**Why:** Ubiquitous in live sound, conference systems, karaoke, portable PA on embedded hardware. Howling = closed-loop high-Q resonances that grow exponentially. Detection: persistent tonal peaks (use dominant-band trackers + pitch + spectral contrast/flatness + hysteresis over seconds; small sorted peak list state). Suppression: insert narrow adaptive notches (parametric biquad or lattice sections from filters scaffold; update coeffs when new howl detected or old dies). State small (list of 4–8 active notch freq/gain/Q + trackers). Traffic: analysis on existing spectrum (zero extra if fused with dominant/pitch/contrast); occasional coeff writes to the filter chain. Cross VAD/heavy features (freeze adaptation during voice). Elegant: reuses almost everything (dominant, pitch, filters, dynamics for level before/after). Fixed-point: notch coeff sensitivity, Q limits.

**Cross-refs:** dominant, pitch, perceptual-sparse (flatness/contrast for tonal detection), filters (notch as lattice/biquad; update), dynamics (pre/post level, freeze), VAD (do not notch on voice), numerical (limit cycles in recirculating notches?).

### 8. Lightweight Time-Varying Effects (Chorus, Flanger, Phaser) via Modulated Fractional Delays
**Proposed:** `algorithms/lightweight-chorus-flanger-phaser-modulated-fractional-delays.md` (or combined with reverb / data_structures)

**Why:** Canonical "wet" effects. Core: read from one or more modulated delay lines (LFO on tap position), mix with dry, optional feedback. Traffic star: every sample does delay-line read(s) + fractional interp (Lagrange or allpass from resampling scaffold) + write. State = delay line(s) + LFO phase + filter state. Can be surprisingly low (few KiB lines for 10–50 ms). Fixed-point: saturation in feedback, interpolation error. Perfect tie to data_structures (circular multi-tap), resampling (frac), filters (allpass for phaser or cheap frac), dynamics (wet/dry or depth ducking), optimization SIMD (parallel reads).

**Cross-refs:** resampling (Lagrange/Thiran/allpass frac), data_structures (rings + multi-tap), filters (allpass sections for phaser or WDF), dynamics (mod depth or mix via envelope), numerical (limit cycles in recirculating), STFT (for "chorus" via phase manip alternative — compare traffic).

### 9. Karplus-Strong & Simple Delay-Line Physical Modeling Primitives
**Proposed:** `algorithms/karplus-strong-and-delay-line-physical-modeling-traffic.md`

**Why:** Ultra-elegant for plucked/string/percussive synthesis on embedded: recirculating delay line (length ≈ period or tuned via frac) + simple averaging/low-pass in the loop for natural decay. One read + write + 1–2 MACs per sample per voice. Traffic = compulsory delay-line access (the archetype "memory bandwidth for synthesis"). Multiple voices = multiple lines or shared with clever scheduling. Fixed-point: bit growth in loop, damping to control DC/limit cycles. Ties directly to reverb (comb filters are related), resampling (tuning), filters (loop filter as biquad/lattice), data_structures (the delay line). High educational + practical value (simple synth engines, modal effects, "string" resonances in reverb).

**Cross-refs:** resampling, filters, data_structures, reverb note (comb as building block), dynamics (amplitude envelope on excitation or loop gain), numerical (stability in recirculating loops).

### 10. Additional Algorithm Proposals (Condensed)
- **Simple dereverberation primitives** (late reverb estimate via exponential decay model or long-term prediction on subbands; spectral subtract or inverse filtering on late part; reuse VAD/NS machinery). Traffic dominated by delay or long smoothing.
- **Phase-vocoder considerations for real-time modification** (expand WSOLA note or separate: extra iFFT + phase manipulation + OLA vs. time-domain WSOLA/PSOLA; when the extra traffic for better quality or formant preservation is justified; cross STFT).
- **Multiband dynamics / de-esser / auto-ducking** (per-band envelopes from existing dynamics + crossover from filters/resampling; side-chain from another envelope or feature). State = N_bands × ballistic words. Fused from existing notes but deserves explicit traffic / state / "how to partition bands without extra DRAM" treatment.
- **General low-state adaptive processing patterns** (LMS-family per subband or on sparse bins; leakage, double-talk, freeze from VAD/dynamics; unification with AEC/feedback/NS).
