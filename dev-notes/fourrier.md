  Is the audit right?

  Yes. The numbers and the grading are fair. Two factual refinements:

  - "pan doesn't use SIMD" is too strong. pan's design has a two-tier Compute HAL, and the primary tier already is portable @Vector(W,T) SIMD — it's src/simd.zig, and the Numeric trait carries a comptime-per-target SIMD width W
  (std.simd.suggestVectorLength). The gain/biquad/pan kernels in the other bench run through it (that's why the dsp_chain bench hits ~200 M frames/s). What's scalar is specifically my P8 FFT butterflies and the resampler FIR — I wrote those without
  @Vector. So it's not "no SIMD in pan," it's "no SIMD in the new spectral kernels yet."
  - Everything else (C-tier raw FFT, A-tier realtime headroom, the full-complex-on-real-input ~2× waste, the missing twiddle LUT, the static-footprint A) matches what the code does.

  Why isn't it SIMD-accelerated — is it deferred in the specs?

  Explicitly, yes. The HAL spec (pan_io_realtime_and_pipeline.md §11, mirrored in catalog.md and pan_architecture_formalisation.md C4) defines exactly two compute layers:

  ▎ Compute HAL. Primary path = portable comptime @Vector(W,T) SIMD (NEON/Helium/AVX) … accelerated paths (runtime-discovered) only for what @Vector doesn't cover well: Accelerate/vDSP (FFT, large convolution) on Apple Silicon; FFTW on x86; CMSIS-DSP on
  ▎ Cortex-M. FFT is the main reason to keep a runtime-discovered accel slot.

  So the spec literally singles out FFT as the canonical thing that lives behind a deferred, optional, runtime-discovered accel slot — not in the portable core. The P8 handoff's "read first" list and the plan both point at "skill ch.04 (FFT-adjacent
  kernels; the optional vDSP/FFTW accel slot)." The audit's "deferred by plan" is accurate.

  Three reasons it's structured this way, all spec-grounded:

  1. P8's deliverable is the rate-elastic seam, not a tuned FFT. The plan's gate is the Rate contract + per-rate-domain PDC + dual-mux + latency-contract. The FFT is just the vehicle that exercises a real type-changing, latency-bearing Rate block.
  Correctness and portability are load-bearing there; raw FFT MHz is not — that's the accel slot's job.
  2. Portability mandate (the brief). pan must run on Apple Silicon, Linux, and FPU-less embedded — the freestanding ReleaseSmall smoke gate compiles the commit pass with no libm/SIMD assumptions. A scalar portable radix-2 is the correct-reference
  baseline that builds on every target; vDSP/FFTW/CMSIS is a per-target overlay added behind the HAL seam without changing the block's observable contract (the Yoneda invariance the whole architecture rests on — same behavior, different execution).
  3. Realtime headroom is the metric pan optimizes for, and it's already A. Stft+iStft ≈ 50 µs is ~0.5% of the 10.67 ms / 512-frame budget → ~200× realtime for one stream. Scalar is already realtime-capable; SIMD/vDSP only starts mattering at high
  channel/instance counts — precisely when you reach for the deferred slot.

  What's not in the specs

  rfft (real-input FFT specialization) — the audit's "single biggest free win (~2×)" — is not named in the specs. It's a pure numerical optimization (no architectural/contract change), so it'd be a legitimate, low-risk future enhancement to the spectral
  kernel rather than a spec item. Vectorizing the butterflies via the existing Compute HAL (@Vector + a twiddle LUT) is likewise on-the-table and on-plan, not a regression.

  Bottom line: the audit's grading is fair and its "the slowness is concentrated exactly where the spec deferred it" conclusion is correct. The one correction is that SIMD is pan's primary compute path — I just didn't vectorize the brand-new spectral
  kernels in this phase, which is consistent with the spec parking FFT acceleration in the runtime-discovered accel slot. I deliberately spent P8's effort on the seam correctness (the gate) over FFT throughput.
